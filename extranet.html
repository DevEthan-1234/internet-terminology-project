Extranet

What is an Extranet?

An Extranet is a controlled private network that allows access to partners, vendors, suppliers, or an authorized set of customers. Essentially, it is a subset of an organization's Intranet that is extended to users outside the company, usually via the public Internet using secure protocols.

Key Characteristics

Extranets are defined by their balance between security and accessibility:

  • Controlled Access: Only specific external users with credentials can enter.
  • Security: Often uses Firewalls, Digital Certificates, and VPNs (Virtual Private Networks) to protect data.
  • Collaboration: Used for sharing inventory data, project updates, or private documentation with business partners.

Extranet vs. Intranet

While an Intranet is strictly for internal employees to share company news and tools, an Extranet opens a digital "window" for outsiders. For example, a car manufacturer might have an Intranet for its engineers, but an Extranet for its global dealerships to order spare parts and track shipments.
