Intranet

What is an Intranet?

An Intranet is a private network used by an organization to securely share company information and computing resources among its employees. Unlike the public Internet, an Intranet is protected by a firewall and is only accessible to authorized internal users.

Common Uses of an Intranet

Organizations implement Intranets to improve internal communication and efficiency. Common features include:

  • Document Management: Storing internal policies, forms, and manuals.
  • Collaboration Tools: Employee directories, internal forums, and project management hubs.
  • Internal Portals: Access to payroll information, leave requests, and training materials.

Security and Infrastructure

An Intranet uses the same protocols as the public Internet (like TCP/IP and HTTP), but it is physically or logically separated from the outside world. It relies heavily on authentication (usernames and passwords) and Firewalls to ensure that sensitive company data never leaks into the public domain.
