A Brief History of the Internet

The journey of the internet began in the late 1960s with ARPANET, a project funded by the U.S. Department of Defense. It was the first network to implement the TCP/IP protocol suite, which became the foundation of the modern web.

By 1989, Sir Tim Berners-Lee invented the World Wide Web while working at CERN, introducing the first browser and web server. Since then, we have evolved from simple static text (Web 1.0) to a global, intelligent, and decentralized ecosystem that powers every aspect of modern life.
